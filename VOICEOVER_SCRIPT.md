## 4. Voice Direction for ElevenLabs

- **Persona:** a calm, confident industrial narrator. Think a senior engineer or a
  premium product film, not an ad.
- **Pace:** medium, ~130-140 words per minute. Unhurried; let technical terms land.
- **Articulation:** clear and precise, especially the term lists (weighing, mixing,
  milling, calendering, curing, inspection / inspection plans, bill of process...).
- **Tone:** warm but technical. Credible and grounded; visionary on the bigger idea,
  honest on the prototype.
- **Energy:** slight cinematic lift on the opening line and the closing line; even
  and measured through the middle.
- **Avoid:** salesy upspeak, over-dramatization, fake excitement, hard sell.
- **Suggested ElevenLabs settings (starting point):** a deep, neutral male or
  warm-authoritative voice; Stability ~50%, Similarity ~75%, Style/Exaggeration low
  (~5-15%), Speaker Boost on. Adjust Stability up if delivery feels uneven.

## 5. Audio export recommendation

- **Format:** WAV (best for editing/sync) or high-bitrate MP3 (192-320 kbps) for a
  smaller file.
- **Sample rate:** 48 kHz preferred (matches video); 44.1 kHz is fine.
- **Channels:** mono is fine for a single narrator (or stereo, your call).
- **No background music in the voice file for now** - keep narration clean and dry;
  add music on a separate track during the edit.
- **Pauses:** leave ~0.5-1.0s of silence between paragraphs (the blank lines above)
  so each scene's line can be placed independently. A short lead-in (~0.5s) and a
  ~2s tail of silence at the end help the closing card breathe.
